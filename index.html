About

Curiosity, in practice.

I’m a graduate student in Information Technology with a habit of taking things apart to understand how they work—whether that’s a codebase, a dataset, or a rough idea for a product.

My work sits at the intersection of full-stack engineering and applied AI. I care less about using the newest tool and more about whether the thing I’m building actually helps someone: a patient tracking their care plan, a researcher wading through papers, a student trying to find a study group.

I’m happiest early in a problem, when the shape of the solution isn’t obvious yet—and I try to bring the same care to the last 10% of polish that I bring to the first idea.
